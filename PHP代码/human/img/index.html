iHMS is a database integrating human histone modification data covering diverse developmental stages and primary tissues. It also incorporates genome-wide expression data of different conditions and reference genes, GC content and CpG island information. iHMS enables users to easily query the states of specific histone modifications in any interested genomic regions through intutive interface, to visualize multiple genome-wide histone modification maps and related expression profiles at different developmental stages and tissues via an powerful browser, and to download data for follow-up analysis.

iHMS Summary

Data Source
All sequencing reads can be accessed from the Sequence Read Archive (SRA) under accession number SRP000941. The raw datasets were produced by the NIH Roadmap Epigenomics Mapping Consortium.

Reference
1.Xie, W., Schultz, M. D., Lister, R., et al. (2013). Epigenomic analysis of multilineage differentiation of human embryonic stem cells. Cell, 153(5), 1134-1148.
2.Bernstein, B. E., Stamatoyannopoulos J A., Costello J F., et al.( 2010) The NIH roadmap epigenomics mapping consortium. Nature biotechnology, 28(10): 10
